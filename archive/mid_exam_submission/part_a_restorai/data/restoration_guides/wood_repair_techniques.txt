Category: Repair Methods
Priority: High

=== WATER RING REMOVAL ===
Material: Wood (Lacquer, Shellac, Varnish finishes)
Damage Type: Water Rings, White Marks
Difficulty: Easy

Water rings appear as white or cloudy marks on wooden surfaces when moisture penetrates the finish but not the wood itself.

Method 1: Iron and Cloth Technique
1. Place a clean, dry cotton cloth over the water ring
2. Set iron to lowest heat setting (no steam)
3. Gently press iron on cloth for 3-5 seconds
4. Lift and check progress
5. Repeat until mark disappears
6. Apply furniture polish to restore shine

Method 2: Mayonnaise Treatment
1. Apply real mayonnaise (full-fat) to the affected area
2. Let sit for 4-8 hours or overnight
3. Wipe clean with soft cloth
4. Buff with furniture polish

Method 3: Baking Soda Paste
1. Mix equal parts white toothpaste (non-gel) and baking soda
2. Rub gently in circular motion with soft cloth
3. Wipe clean after 1-2 minutes
4. Apply wood polish

WARNING: Do NOT use these methods on waxed finishes or unfinished wood.

=== SCRATCH REPAIR ===
Material: Solid Wood, Veneer
Damage Type: Surface Scratches, Deep Gouges
Difficulty: Medium

Surface Scratches (finish only):
1. Clean area with mineral spirits
2. Use matching furniture marker or walnut meat rubbed along scratch
3. Apply paste wax and buff
4. For deeper color, use iodine (for dark woods) or tea (for medium woods)

Deep Scratches (into wood):
1. Clean scratch thoroughly
2. Fill with colored wood filler or shellac stick
3. Heat shellac stick with alcohol lamp
4. Drop melted shellac into scratch
5. Level with razor blade when cool
6. Sand with 400-grit paper
7. Apply matching finish

=== VENEER REPAIR ===
Material: Veneer
Damage Type: Bubbling, Lifting, Missing pieces
Difficulty: Medium-Hard

Reattaching Loose Veneer:
1. Clean under loose veneer with thin blade
2. Apply yellow wood glue with syringe or thin stick
3. Cover with wax paper
4. Clamp with wood blocks and even pressure
5. Leave clamped for 24 hours
6. Remove excess glue with damp cloth

Replacing Missing Veneer:
1. Cut donor veneer slightly larger than damaged area
2. Tape over damaged section
3. Cut through both layers simultaneously for perfect match
4. Remove old veneer carefully
5. Apply contact cement to both surfaces
6. Press new veneer firmly
7. Roll with veneer roller or use clamp
8. Trim and sand edges

WARNING: Always work with grain direction. Never force lifted veneer.
