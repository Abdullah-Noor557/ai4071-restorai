FURNITURE RESTORATION GUIDE - SAFETY INFORMATION & HAZARDOUS MATERIALS
Source: Occupational Safety and Restoration Best Practices
Last Updated: February 2026
Category: Safety
Priority: CRITICAL

=== GENERAL SAFETY RULES ===

ALWAYS:
✓ Work in well-ventilated areas
✓ Wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE)
✓ Keep fire extinguisher nearby when using flammable materials
✓ Read and follow all product safety data sheets (SDS)
✓ Keep materials away from heat sources and open flames
✓ Store chemicals in original containers with labels
✓ Dispose of materials according to local regulations

NEVER:
✗ Mix chemicals unless specifically instructed
✗ Eat, drink, or smoke while working with chemicals
✗ Work near open flames with flammable solvents
✗ Pour chemicals down drain without checking regulations
✗ Use damaged power tools or frayed electrical cords

=== PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT ===

MINIMUM PPE Requirements:
- Safety glasses or goggles (chemical-rated for strippers)
- Nitrile or chemical-resistant gloves
- Dust mask or respirator (N95 minimum for sanding)
- Work apron or old clothing
- Closed-toe shoes

ENHANCED PPE (for chemical stripping, spray finishing):
- Full-face respirator with organic vapor cartridges
- Chemical-resistant gloves (neoprene or nitrile)
- Long sleeves and pants
- Ventilation fan or outdoor work area

=== CHEMICAL HAZARDS ===

CHEMICAL STRIPPERS (Methylene Chloride, NMP-based):
Material: Paint and finish removal
Hazard Level: HIGH
Risks: Skin burns, respiratory irritation, neurotoxicity

Safety Measures:
- Use ONLY in outdoor or well-ventilated areas
- Wear chemical-resistant gloves (neoprene)
- Wear goggles or face shield
- Avoid skin contact completely
- If contact occurs: rinse with water for 15+ minutes
- Keep away from heat and flames

Safer Alternative: Citrus-based strippers (slower but less toxic)

MINERAL SPIRITS / PAINT THINNER:
Material: Cleaning, thinning oil-based finishes
Hazard Level: MEDIUM
Risks: Flammable, respiratory irritation, skin irritation

Safety Measures:
- Use in ventilated area
- Keep away from sparks and flames
- Wear gloves
- Store in metal safety can with tight lid
- Dispose of rags properly (spontaneous combustion risk)

DENATURED ALCOHOL:
Material: Shellac solvent, cleaning
Hazard Level: MEDIUM
Risks: Highly flammable, toxic if ingested, eye irritant

Safety Measures:
- Keep away from heat sources and flames
- Work in ventilated area
- Wear gloves and safety glasses
- Do not ingest
- Store in original container away from heat

LACQUER THINNER:
Material: Lacquer application and cleanup
Hazard Level: HIGH
Risks: Extremely flammable, neurotoxic, respiratory irritant

Safety Measures:
- Use only in well-ventilated area or outdoors
- Wear organic vapor respirator
- Keep away from all ignition sources
- Chemical-resistant gloves required
- Avoid prolonged exposure

WOOD STAINS (Oil-based):
Material: Coloring wood
Hazard Level: MEDIUM
Risks: Flammable, fumes can cause dizziness

Safety Measures:
- Use in ventilated area
- Wear gloves
- Avoid prolonged skin contact
- Store away from heat

POLYURETHANE (Oil-based):
Material: Top coat finish
Hazard Level: MEDIUM
Risks: Flammable, strong fumes, respiratory irritation

Safety Measures:
- Apply in well-ventilated area
- Wear respirator for large projects
- Keep away from heat sources
- Allow proper drying/curing time

=== SPONTANEOUS COMBUSTION RISK ===

WARNING: Oil-soaked rags are a FIRE HAZARD

Materials at Risk:
- Linseed oil
- Tung oil
- Oil-based stains and finishes
- Danish oil
- Any drying oils

Why Dangerous:
Drying oils generate heat through oxidation. Bunched rags can trap heat, reaching ignition temperature (causing fire WITHOUT external flame).

PROPER DISPOSAL:
Method 1 (Safest):
1. Spread rags flat outdoors on non-combustible surface
2. Allow to dry completely (24-48 hours)
3. Dispose in regular trash once fully dry and stiff

Method 2:
1. Place wet rags in metal container with tight lid
2. Fill completely with water
3. Keep outdoors away from buildings
4. Take to hazardous waste facility

NEVER:
✗ Pile oil-soaked rags together
✗ Put in regular trash while still wet
✗ Store in closed containers without water
✗ Leave in garage or workshop overnight

=== DUST HAZARDS ===

Wood Dust Health Risks:
- Respiratory irritation
- Allergic reactions
- Increased cancer risk with prolonged exposure (especially hardwoods)
- Eye irritation

Protection:
- Wear N95 or better dust mask
- Use dust collection system when power sanding
- Sand outdoors when possible
- Wear safety glasses
- Damp-mop work area after sanding

Exotic Woods (Higher Risk):
- Walnut: Can cause severe allergic reactions
- Cocobolo, Rosewood: Respiratory sensitizers
- Cedar: Asthma trigger for some individuals

=== TOOL SAFETY ===

Power Sanders:
- Secure work piece before sanding
- Keep cord away from sanding surface
- Let tool come to full stop before setting down
- Use dust collection

Heat Guns:
- Never leave unattended while on
- Point away from yourself and flammable materials
- Can ignite materials if held too close
- Allow to cool before storing

Spray Equipment:
- Follow manufacturer guidelines
- Wear respirator rated for spray finishing
- Work in spray booth or outdoors
- Ground all equipment to prevent static sparks

=== FIRST AID ===

Chemical Splash to Eyes:
1. Rinse with water for 15-20 minutes
2. Hold eyelids open while rinsing
3. Seek medical attention immediately

Chemical on Skin:
1. Remove contaminated clothing
2. Rinse affected area with water for 15+ minutes
3. Wash with soap and water
4. Seek medical attention if irritation persists

Inhalation of Fumes:
1. Move to fresh air immediately
2. Sit or lie down
3. Loosen tight clothing
4. Seek medical attention if symptoms persist

Ingestion:
1. Do NOT induce vomiting
2. Call poison control immediately: 1-800-222-1222
3. Have product container/label available

=== ENVIRONMENTAL DISPOSAL ===

Never Pour Down Drain:
- Paint strippers
- Mineral spirits
- Lacquer thinner
- Oil-based stains or finishes

Proper Disposal:
- Take to household hazardous waste facility
- Check local municipality for collection days
- Some paint stores accept leftover finishes

Safe to Dispose (after fully dry):
- Water-based products
- Completely dried rags
- Sawdust (except from pressure-treated or toxic woods)

=== WORKSPACE SAFETY ===

Fire Prevention:
- Keep flammable materials away from heat sources
- No smoking in work area
- Have fire extinguisher rated for chemical fires (Class B)
- Know evacuation route

Electrical Safety:
- Use GFCI outlets in damp areas
- Inspect cords for damage before use
- Don't overload circuits
- Unplug tools when changing accessories

Ventilation:
- Open windows and doors
- Use exhaust fans (explosion-proof for spray finishing)
- Work outdoors when possible for chemical use
- Use respirator when ventilation is insufficient

=== RESTRICTED/BANNED MATERIALS ===

DO NOT USE (Health Hazards):
✗ Lead-based paints (banned, but may exist on old furniture)
✗ Asbestos-containing materials
✗ Chromated copper arsenate (CCA) treated wood for indoor projects
✗ Formaldehyde-releasing adhesives without proper ventilation

If Suspected:
- Do not sand or disturb
- Consult professional for testing
- May require professional abatement
