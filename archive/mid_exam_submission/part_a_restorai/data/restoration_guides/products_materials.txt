FURNITURE RESTORATION GUIDE - PRODUCTS & MATERIALS REFERENCE
Source: Professional Restorer's Supply Guide
Last Updated: January 2026
Category: Products
Priority: Medium

=== ADHESIVES ===

WOOD GLUE (PVA - Polyvinyl Acetate):
Brand Examples: Titebond, Elmer's Wood Glue
Material: Solid wood joints, general woodworking
Strength: High
Dry Time: Clamp 30-60 minutes, full cure 24 hours
Application: Interior use only, not water-resistant (except Type II and III)
Best for: Chair repairs, joint reconstruction, edge gluing

HIDE GLUE (Traditional):
Forms: Liquid (pre-mixed), Granules (mix with water), Hot (heated pot)
Material: Antique furniture, veneer, traditional restoration
Strength: Strong, reversible
Dry Time: 2-4 hours clamp time
Application: Heat granules to 145°F, apply warm
Best for: Museum-quality restoration, period-correct repairs, easy reversibility
Advantages: Reversible with heat/moisture, traditional, long open time

EPOXY:
Types: 5-minute, 30-minute, slow-set (2-hour)
Material: Structural repairs, gap-filling, rotted wood stabilization
Strength: Extremely high
Dry Time: Varies by formula
Application: Mix two parts, apply immediately
Best for: Permanent repairs, outdoor furniture, load-bearing joints
Warning: NOT reversible, difficult to sand

CONTACT CEMENT:
Brand Examples: DAP Weldwood, Barge
Material: Veneer application, laminate
Strength: Immediate bond
Dry Time: Apply to both surfaces, wait until tacky (15-20 min), press together
Application: No repositioning possible after contact
Best for: Large veneer sheets, plastic laminate

CYANOACRYLATE (CA Glue / Super Glue):
Viscosity: Thin, medium, thick
Material: Small chips, crack filling with accelerator
Strength: Very high for small bonds
Dry Time: Seconds with accelerator
Application: Apply to one surface, mist accelerator on other
Best for: Instant repairs, stabilizing small cracks, small parts

=== WOOD FILLERS ===

WOOD PUTTY (Solvent-based):
Brand Examples: Minwax Wood Putty
Material: Finished wood only
Use: Filling nail holes, small gaps
Shrinkage: Minimal
Sandable: Yes
Stainable: No (use only on finished surfaces)
Colors: Multiple wood tones available
Dry Time: 15-30 minutes

WOOD FILLER (Water-based):
Brand Examples: Elmer's Wood Filler, DAP Plastic Wood
Material: Bare or finished wood
Use: Larger holes, gouges, imperfections
Shrinkage: Some shrinkage (may need second application)
Sandable: Yes
Stainable: Poorly (limited uptake)
Dry Time: 30 minutes to 2 hours
Application: Apply with putty knife, overfill slightly

COLORED WOOD FILLER:
Brand Examples: Famowood, Plastic Wood
Colors: Pre-mixed to match wood species
Use: Repairs where close color match needed
Stainable: No
Best for: Nail holes, gouges in finished pieces

TWO-PART EPOXY FILLER:
Brand Examples: Abatron WoodEpox, System Three
Material: Structural repairs, rotted wood replacement
Use: Large voids, outdoor furniture, structural damage
Sandable: Yes (but difficult, very hard)
Stainable: No
Strength: Extremely high
Application: Mix two parts, sculpt into place
Dry Time: 30 minutes to 6 hours depending on formula

SHELLAC STICK (Burn-in Stick):
Colors: Wide range matching wood tones
Material: Finished furniture only
Use: Deep scratches, small gouges in finish
Application: Melt with alcohol lamp or burn-in knife, flow into damage
Sandable: Yes, when cool
Best for: Professional-looking repairs without refinishing

=== STRIPPERS & CLEANERS ===

CHEMICAL STRIPPER - Methylene Chloride Based:
Brand Examples: Klean-Strip, Jasco
Material: All finishes
Strength: Very aggressive, fast
Hazard: High - neurotoxic, requires excellent ventilation
Application: Brush on, wait 15-30 minutes, scrape
Best for: Multiple finish layers, difficult stripping jobs
Disposal: Hazardous waste facility

SAFER STRIPPER - Biochemical/Citrus Based:
Brand Examples: Citristrip, Soy-Gel
Material: Most finishes (slower on polyurethane)
Strength: Moderate, requires longer dwell time
Hazard: Low - better for indoor use
Application: Apply thick coat, wait 30 minutes to 24 hours
Best for: Indoor projects, health-conscious users

MINERAL SPIRITS (Paint Thinner):
Grade: Regular, Odorless
Use: Cleaning brushes, thinning oil-based finishes, degreasing
Hazard: Flammable, moderate fumes
Application: Wipe or brush on
Best for: Final cleaning before finishing, removing wax

DENATURED ALCOHOL:
Use: Shellac thinner, cleaning, removing alcohol-based finishes
Hazard: Flammable, toxic if ingested
Application: Wipe or brush
Best for: Shellac work, cleaning old shellac finishes

LACQUER THINNER:
Use: Thinning lacquer, cleaning spray guns, removing lacquer
Hazard: High - very flammable, strong fumes
Application: Spray equipment cleaning, finish removal
Best for: Lacquer finishing projects only

TSP (Trisodium Phosphate):
Use: Heavy cleaning, paint prep, removing grime
Form: Powder, mix with water
Application: Scrub with solution, rinse thoroughly
Best for: Cleaning old furniture before refinishing
Warning: Wear gloves, caustic to skin

OXALIC ACID (Wood Bleach):
Use: Removing water stains, ink stains, rust stains from wood
Form: Powder or crystals, mix with water
Application: Apply solution, let dry, neutralize with baking soda solution
Best for: Dark water stains that penetrate wood
Warning: Wear gloves and eye protection

=== ABRASIVES ===

SANDPAPER:
Grits: 60 (coarse) to 320 (very fine)
Backing: Paper (hand sanding), cloth (power sanding), foam (contours)
Types: Aluminum oxide (general), garnet (hand sanding), silicon carbide (wet sanding)
Progression: 80 → 120 → 150 → 220 (typical furniture refinishing)

STEEL WOOL:
Grades: #4 (coarse) to #0000 (super fine)
Use: Stripping residue removal, applying wax, dulling gloss
Common Grades:
  #0000 - Applying wax, final finish smoothing
  #00 - Light cleaning between finish coats
  #0 - Removing old finish residue
  #1 - Moderate cleaning, rust removal
Warning: Don't use on wet water-based finishes (rust stains)

SYNTHETIC STEEL WOOL:
Brand: Scotch-Brite pads
Use: Alternative to steel wool, won't rust
Best for: Water-based finishes, won't leave rust specs

SANDING SPONGES:
Grits: Medium, Fine, Super Fine
Use: Contoured surfaces, shaped edges
Advantage: Washable, reusable, conforms to shape

=== FINISHING PRODUCTS ===

SHELLAC:
Forms: Pre-mixed liquid, flakes (mix your own)
Cuts: 1 lb, 2 lb, 3 lb (pounds of shellac per gallon of alcohol)
Colors: Clear, amber, garnet, blonde
Dry Time: 2-3 hours
Recoat: 2-3 hours
Application: Brush or pad
Advantages: Traditional, repairable, seals odors

POLYURETHANE - Oil-Based:
Brands: Minwax, Varathane
Sheen: Gloss, semi-gloss, satin, matte
Dry Time: 24 hours between coats
Cure Time: 72 hours minimum
Application: Brush, high-quality natural bristle
Advantages: Very durable, water-resistant

POLYURETHANE - Water-Based:
Brands: Minwax Polycrylic, General Finishes
Sheen: Gloss, semi-gloss, satin, matte
Dry Time: 2-3 hours between coats
Cure Time: 48 hours
Application: Brush, synthetic bristle or foam
Advantages: Low odor, clear (no yellowing), fast dry

TUNG OIL:
Forms: Pure, polymerized (faster drying)
Dry Time: 24 hours between coats
Total Coats: 4-6
Application: Wipe on, wipe off excess
Advantages: Natural, enhances grain, hand-rubbed look

DANISH OIL:
Composition: Mix of oil and varnish
Brands: Watco, Tried & True
Dry Time: 8-12 hours
Application: Flood surface, keep wet 15-30 minutes, wipe excess
Advantages: Easy application, penetrating finish

LACQUER:
Forms: Spray only (for hobbyist)
Dry Time: 30 minutes between coats
Coats: 3-4 thin coats
Application: HVLP spray gun
Advantages: Beautiful finish, fast drying, repairable
Warning: Requires spray setup, strong fumes

PASTE WAX:
Brands: Johnson's, Briwax, Howard
Use: Final protective layer over sealed finish
Application: Apply thin coat, let haze, buff
Reapplication: Every 3-6 months
Advantages: Enhances sheen, protective, traditional

=== STAINS ===

OIL-BASED STAIN:
Brands: Minwax Wood Finish, Varathane
Colors: Hundreds of options
Dry Time: 6-8 hours before topcoat
Application: Brush or cloth, wipe excess
Advantages: Long working time, deep penetration

GEL STAIN:
Brands: General Finishes, Java Gel Stain
Colors: Limited but rich selection
Application: Wipe on with cloth
Best for: Vertical surfaces, pine, maple (blotch-prone woods)
Advantages: No drips, even color

WATER-BASED STAIN:
Brands: General Finishes, Minwax Water-Based
Dry Time: 2-3 hours
Application: Brush or cloth
Advantages: Low odor, fast dry, soap and water cleanup
Warning: Raises grain, requires extra sanding

DYE STAINS:
Forms: Powder (mix with water or alcohol)
Use: Even coloring, color correction
Application: Spray or wipe
Advantages: No grain obscuring, even color on blotchy woods
Requires: Top coat for protection

=== SPECIALTY PRODUCTS ===

WOOD CONDITIONER:
Brands: Minwax Pre-Stain, Charles Neil
Use: Apply before staining pine, maple, cherry
Purpose: Prevents blotching, ensures even stain absorption
Application: Apply, wait 15 minutes, lightly sand, stain while slightly damp

GRAIN FILLER:
Use: Filling open pores of oak, ash, mahogany
Purpose: Glass-smooth finish on open-grain woods
Application: Apply across grain, scrape excess, sand when dry

SANDING SEALER:
Use: First coat before topcoat finish
Purpose: Faster build, easier sanding than topcoat
Application: Brush or spray, sand with 320-grit

RUBBING COMPOUND:
Use: Smoothing final finish
Grit: 1000-2000 grit equivalent
Application: Apply with cloth, rub in circular motion
Result: Satin to gloss sheen

POLISHING COMPOUND:
Use: After rubbing compound for high gloss
Grit: 3000+ grit equivalent
Application: Buff with soft cloth
Result: Mirror-like finish

FURNITURE POLISH:
Types: Spray, liquid, cream
Use: Maintenance cleaning and shine
Common: Pledge, Howard Feed-N-Wax, Old English
Application: Spray and wipe, or apply with cloth

=== TOOLS & SUPPLIES ===

BRUSHES:
Natural Bristle: Use with oil-based finishes
Synthetic Bristle: Use with water-based finishes
Sizes: 1", 2", 3" (most common for furniture)
Quality: Higher quality = fewer brush marks

FOAM BRUSHES:
Use: Disposable, water or oil-based
Best for: Small projects, touch-ups
Limitation: Can leave bubbles in finish

RAGS & APPLICATORS:
Cotton T-shirt Material: Best for staining and oil application
Tack Cloth: Pre-finish dust removal
Paper Shop Towels: General cleanup

SCRAPERS:
Paint Scraper: Removing old finish
Cabinet Scraper: Smoothing wood (alternative to sanding)
Plastic Scraper: Removing stripper without damaging wood

CLAMPS:
Bar Clamps: Long joints, tabletops
C-Clamps: General purpose
Spring Clamps: Quick small jobs
Band Clamps: Chairs, irregular shapes
